Introduction

Congkak, also known by various other names across Southeast Asia such as Congklak, Sungka, or Dakon is a traditional mancala-style game that has roots in various cultures within the religion. It is a two-player game that involves controlling a row of small houses (small hole) and a larger storehouse (large hole) at their respective ends of the board.

Congkak requires tactical thinking, planning, and careful execution of moves to outplay the opponent and accumulate more seeds in the storehouse by the end of the game. Congkak is not only a source of entertainment but also holds cultural significance in the regions where it is played, often passed down through generations as a traditional pastime. It fosters social interaction, strategic thinking, and mathematical skills among players.

If you're looking for something fun to play, congkak is a classic Malaysian game that's easy to learn. While congkak looks and plays similar to mancala, it introduces distinct rules that require consideration before initiating the game. Stay tuned as we will guide you through the gameplay mechanics, including board setup and executing your moves.

Objective

  • Accumulate more seeds in your storehouse (large hole) than your opponent.
  • Distribute seeds strategically across the board and capture seeds from your opponent's side.
  • Aim to gather the most seeds into your storehouse by the end of the game.
  • Win by having the highest number of seeds in your storehouse when the game concludes.

Game Setup

Congkak Board

The Congkak board consists of two rows of seven holes or cups, with one larger storehouse (big hole) at each end. The holes or cups also known as "lubang" in Malay.

Seeds or Marbles

A total of 98 seeds or marbles are used. The seeds should be evenly distributed among the smaller holes on the board, which 7 seeds placed in each of the 14 smaller holes at the start of the game.

Positioning of Players

Each player positions themselves in front of their row of houses, with their storehouse located on their left side.

Gameplay

Step 1
Two players position themselves in front of a row of houses, ensuring that their storehouse is situated on their left.
Fill each of the houses on the congkak board with 7 pieces each.

Step 2
When it is on your turn, choose one of the 7 houses in the row closest to you. Take out all the pieces and go clockwise around the board, dropping 1 piece into each house.
Make sure to drop 1 piece into your storehouse when you pass it, but skip over your opponent's storehouse.

Step 3
Continue your turn when you drop your last piece in a house with other pieces
If you drop the last piece in your storehouse, choose any other house on your side of the board and take another turn

Step 4
If you drop your final piece into one of your vacant houses, place that piece along with all the pieces in the house directly into your storehouse.
If the house on your opponent's side of the board is empty, place your last piece into your storehouse.

Step 5
You will lose your turn if you put your last piece in one of your opponent's empty house.
So, plan wisely your moves to aim for landing in a house containing other pieces.

Step 6
Win if you have more pieces in your storehouse at the last of the game.

Strategy Tip

Select houses during your turn that enable you to execute multiple consecutive moves.
Count the number of pieces within a house before selecting it to determine the final destination of the last piece.
